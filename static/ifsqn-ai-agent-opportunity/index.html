Scoring method. I re-scored the candidates using four signals: forum evidence of pain, amount of cross-document work, suitability for a multi-step AI agent, and whether the result can be kept verifiable with source links and human approval. High scores do not mean "let AI decide"; they mean "an agent can reduce busywork while preserving traceability and control."

Key change from the earlier ranking. Management review, HACCP change-impact, and food-fraud horizon scanning move up under an agent lens because they involve gathering information from many places, monitoring for triggers, summarising evidence, and producing structured follow-up actions.

Best AI-Agent Patterns

Evidence-Gathering Agent

Pulls records, extracts fields, builds evidence indexes, identifies missing proof, and drafts an audit pack. Best fit: traceability, management review, supplier approval, internal audit.

Monitoring Agent

Watches dates, certificates, trends, alerts, supplier changes, complaint spikes, lab trends and horizon-scanning sources. Best fit: supplier approval, food fraud, environmental monitoring, KPIs.

Impact-Analysis Agent

When something changes, checks related specifications, labels, HACCP plans, supplier approvals, allergen matrices and records, then recommends a human-reviewed action plan.

Where Agents Are Less Differentiated

Medium Fit

Specification manager, raw-material risk assessment, HACCP source-evidence library, process-flow verifier, training matrix, calibration asset manager, site zoning mapper and food-defence assessment can all use AI assistance. The agent value is narrower: extraction, reminders, comparison, summarisation and change-impact prompts.

Lower Fit

Simple registers and checklists such as chemical control, waste records, surplus food decisions, glass breakage, sharps registers, personal hygiene, medical screening, protective clothing and stock rotation are better framed as structured workflow software. AI may help with drafting or OCR, but it is not the main product differentiator.

Recommended Agent Product Strategy

Best wedge: start with an evidence-gathering and audit-pack agent, not a free-form compliance chatbot. The strongest product surface is a controlled workflow where the agent reads records, finds gaps, drafts outputs, and preserves source links.

Most defensible first products: management review pack generator, traceability/mock-recall workspace, supplier approval monitor, and HACCP change-impact workflow. These have strong forum pain and clear agent tasks that can be human-approved.

Most agent-native expansion: food fraud horizon scanning. It naturally benefits from ongoing external monitoring, but it also requires careful source quality controls and explicit review before changing vulnerability assessments.
