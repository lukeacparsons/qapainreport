Core findingpaper/ERP hybrid evidence packs

Executive finding. Sites generally solve this problem with a hybrid of procedure, spreadsheet/template, ERP/WMS/accounting exports, paper batch records, copied dispatch evidence, and a timed front-sheet report. Better-equipped sites rely on ERP/SAP/SAGE/WMS and job cards, but even then they still need to assemble source documents, explain variances, verify contacts, and retain an audit pack.

Important nuance. Forum users often blur "traceability exercise", "mass balance", "mock recall", and "recall procedure test". In practice, many sites combine them into one exercise, but the evidence shows they are not the same thing: traceability proves lots and quantities, mass balance proves reconciliation, and mock recall tests people, contacts, decision-making and timing.

Current-State Process Map

1. Pick scenarioFinished lot, raw material lot, packaging lot, supplier alert, customer complaint, random invoice or job.
2. Start timerRecord date, start time, participants, role assignments, and sometimes total completion time.
3. Gather recordsPull batch records, goods-in, COAs, specs, production records, dispatch records, invoices, BOLs and inventory.
4. Trace lotsRun forward and backward tracing across raw materials, packaging, WIP, finished goods, warehouses and customers.
5. Reconcile quantitiesCompare received/issued/used/produced/shipped/stock/waste/rework/sample quantities and explain variance.
6. Check contactsVerify recall team, supplier, customer, regulator or certification-body contact routes, often without alarming customers.
7. Record timelineCapture when complaint/alert was received, team convened, trace completed, contacts checked and decisions made.
8. Build reportCreate a front sheet plus attachments so auditors can review the outcome without digging through raw files.
9. Raise actionsDocument gaps, bottlenecks, missing evidence, contact failures and corrective actions.
10. Retain packFile the exercise as controlled evidence for audit and use the results to improve the procedure.

Evidence-Backed Current Tool Stack

Paper and spreadsheets

Common in smaller sites. Paper receiving records, paper batch records, printed forms, front sheets and spreadsheets remain central. Users report that manual systems can work, but they require discipline and good filing.

ERP/WMS/accounting systems

ERP, SAP, SAGE, WMS and accounting systems make lot lookup faster. They usually do not replace the need to attach evidence, explain mass-balance variance or verify contacts.

Templates and audit packs

Templates are widely requested because people need a consistent way to prove scenario, participants, timing, trace path, mass balance, contacts, deviations, corrective actions and sign-off.

What This Means for a Product

Must-have workflow

  • Scenario builder for raw material, finished product, packaging, complaint, supplier alert or random invoice.
  • Forward/backward trace map with batch, lot, WIP, warehouse, customer and supplier links.
  • Mass-balance worksheet with configurable tolerances and variance explanations.
  • Evidence checklist and source-document attachments.
  • Timeline, recall-team roles, contact verification and decision log.
  • Audit-pack export with front page plus supporting records.

AI-agent opportunities

  • Extract lot, quantity, date, supplier and customer data from PDFs, scans and spreadsheets.
  • Suggest trace links from messy records, while requiring user confirmation.
  • Detect missing evidence and unexplained variance.
  • Draft supplier trace-challenge requests and customer contact scripts.
  • Draft the mock recall report and corrective-action language.
  • Answer audit-style questions over confirmed evidence only.

Open Product Questions From the Evidence

1. Does the product start as standalone or ERP-adjacent? Forum evidence shows both paper-heavy sites and ERP/SAP/SAGE/WMS users. The safest initial shape is a standalone evidence workspace with imports and exports, not a full ERP replacement.

2. Should mock recall and traceability be one product or two? Users blur the terms, but the workflows differ. Product design should present one exercise workspace with separate checklist tabs: traceability, mass balance, recall procedure, contacts and CAPA.

3. How much judgement should the system make? It should calculate and flag; it should not autonomously decide whether a recall is required, whether customer communication is adequate, or whether a mass-balance variance is acceptable without a documented human rationale.
