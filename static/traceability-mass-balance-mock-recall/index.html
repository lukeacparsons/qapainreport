This product is an audit-proof workspace for running traceability tests, mock recalls, and mass-balance exercises. It helps a QA or technical manager prove where a material came from, where it went, what quantities were involved, what evidence supports the conclusion, and what gaps need corrective action.

Important design principle. The traceability engine should be deterministic. Lot links, dates, quantities, mass balance, sign-offs and audit logs need to be rules-based and auditable. AI should reduce manual document work, suggest links, find gaps, draft reports and explain discrepancies, but users must be able to verify every claim against source records.

The Requirement

What the site must prove

  • Forward traceability: raw material, packaging, ingredient or traded-product lot to finished product and customer.
  • Backward traceability: finished product, complaint, dispatch or customer lot back to materials, suppliers and process records.
  • Mass balance: quantities received, used, dispatched, retained, wasted, sampled, reworked or destroyed reconcile.
  • Rework and packaging are included where relevant.
  • The exercise is completed within the site or auditor's expected time window.
  • Evidence is retained and can be reviewed during audit.

Why it is hard in practice

  • Records are spread across goods-in, warehouse, production, QA, rework, dispatch and supplier files.
  • Different teams own different parts of the evidence.
  • Manual spreadsheets do not naturally preserve source links and audit trails.
  • Quantity differences need explanation, not just calculation.
  • Real incidents require speed, while audits require proof.
  • Supplier traceability challenges can expose gaps outside the site.

Concrete Pain Scenario

A flour supplier notifies the site that lot F123 may be contaminated. The QA manager needs to prove which deliveries contained F123, which production batches used it, which finished lots were made, which customers received them, what is still in stock, what was wasted or sampled, whether any rework was involved, whether packaging/labels are traceable, and whether the mass balance reconciles. The records are scattered across goods-in, production, warehouse, QA and dispatch files. The auditor or incident team expects a fast, complete answer.

Product Flows

Flow A: Raw Material Lot to Customers

  • User enters or scans raw material lot.
  • System identifies receipts and supplier records.
  • System links the lot to production batches and rework.
  • Finished lots are traced to dispatches and customers.
  • Mass balance compares receipt quantity with usage, waste, stock and dispatch outcomes.
  • Export answers: "Where did this material go?"

Flow B: Finished Product Lot Backward

  • User starts from a finished product lot, customer complaint or dispatch note.
  • System finds batch records and production date.
  • Ingredients, packaging, process records, CCP checks and supplier lots are linked.
  • Missing COAs or batch records are flagged.
  • Export answers: "What went into this product?"

Flow C: Mock Recall Exercise

  • User selects scenario and starts timer.
  • System records participants, roles and objective.
  • Trace map and mass balance are completed.
  • Recall contacts, customer list and stock hold decisions are captured.
  • Gaps become corrective actions.
  • Export produces an auditor-ready mock recall pack.

Flow D: Supplier Traceability Challenge

  • User selects supplier, material and lot.
  • System sends a structured request for supplier trace evidence.
  • Supplier response is uploaded or entered.
  • System checks completeness against expected evidence.
  • Weak responses create supplier monitoring actions.

Flow E: Quantity Reconciliation

  • System calculates received quantity.
  • System subtracts production use, waste, samples, rework, stock, returns and destruction.
  • Variance is shown as a percentage and absolute quantity.
  • User must explain or correct unexplained differences.
  • Final reconciliation is locked into the test record.

Flow F: CAPA From Trace Gaps

  • System flags missing or weak evidence.
  • User assigns owner, due date and corrective action.
  • Actions link back to the trace test.
  • Effectiveness is reviewed in the next mock recall or internal audit.
  • Recurring gaps are escalated.

How AI Is Used

High-value AI Document extraction

AI reads PDFs, scans, spreadsheets, delivery notes, COAs, batch sheets, dispatch notes and emails to extract supplier, lot, batch, quantity, product, date and customer data. Every extracted value must link back to its source document with confidence scoring.

High-value AI Trace-link suggestions

AI suggests relationships such as "flour lot F123 appears on goods-in GR-1042, batch PB-2219, finished lots FP-8831 and FP-8832, and dispatch notes DN-7780 to DN-7784." The user confirms the links.

High-value AI Missing-evidence checks

AI reviews the test file and flags likely gaps: missing dispatch record, missing supplier trace response, unlinked rework, packaging not included, weak backward traceability or incomplete evidence index.

High-value AI Discrepancy explanation

The rules engine calculates the variance. AI helps interpret notes and records, for example identifying startup waste, QA samples or overfill that may explain part of the mass-balance gap.

Useful AI Report drafting

AI drafts the mock recall report: objective, scenario, timeline, products affected, customers affected, quantity reconciliation, evidence reviewed, gaps found, corrective actions and conclusion.

Useful AI Natural language questions

Users can ask: "Where did flour lot F123 go?", "Which customers received finished lot FP-8832?", "What evidence is missing?", or "Why does the mass balance not reconcile?"

AI Boundaries

Area Should be deterministic AI can assist with
Lot traceability Confirmed links between records, lots, batches and dispatches. Suggesting possible links from messy documents or filenames.
Mass balance Quantity arithmetic, variance percentage, tolerance rules and locked final values. Interpreting notes that explain waste, samples, rework or stock movements.
Evidence Source files, timestamps, user approvals and audit trail. Summarising documents and extracting key fields with source references.
Recall decisions Human decision, documented authorisation and escalation rules. Preparing decision-support summaries and contact lists.
CAPA Owner, due date, approval, closure and effectiveness evidence. Drafting root cause, corrective action and verification wording for human review.

Audit Pack Output

The final export should answer the auditor's likely questions without forcing the QA manager to assemble a folder manually. It should include the test objective, test date, start and end time, participants, scenario, trace direction, affected lots, affected customers, supplier records, production records, rework, stock on hand, dispatches, mass-balance calculation, discrepancies, evidence index, corrective actions, conclusion and sign-off.

Positioning

Clear positioning

AI-assisted traceability testing that finds records, links evidence, checks gaps, reconciles quantities and drafts an auditor-ready mock recall pack.

What not to claim

Do not position it as "AI decides your recall." Recall decisions, regulatory notification and product disposition must remain controlled human decisions with documented approval.
